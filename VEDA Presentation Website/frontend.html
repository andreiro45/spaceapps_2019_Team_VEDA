       H.E.L.I.O.S., meaning hierarchially enabled long distance internet overseas, is a project built with one thing in mind, being able to have access to the internet in a no-cell area, in this case, oceanic areas where there is no connection cell towers in range.

       As developer of the project,VEDA has come up with three main ideas, to allow virtually anyone, that is at sea, to connect to a WiFi transmitter, allowing humanity to have an even greater possibility of surviving the harsh conditions traveling at sea can bring.

       The main three ideas are:
    1. Radios and Antennas (that send signals from the ground to either other ships's own antennas or to balloons).
    2. Mesh Network (which will allow a better connection between the antennas, the ships and the balloons).
    3. Balloons (balloons placed in a precise position in the upper atmoshere that allow omnidirectional signal sending and wifi transmision via an unidirectional antenna that is facing downwords, with the emitter being the shape of a large cone).



  RADIOS AND ANTENNAS

       Initially we wanted to use radio communication between the balloons, but soon, we realised that, at such great altitudes, the balloons would have a clear line of sight between them, air dencity and turbulence would not be a problem.

       Then, we figured we could get much higher data transfer speeds by communicating over an optical link instead of a radio one.

       In the end, we ended up using an initial radio broadcast to figure out which two balloons are closest to each other.   After we aquire both data sets we direct a laser reciever / transmiter pair from both balloons to each other, thus establishing a much more reliable and faster data connection, than radio, between the two.

       By using multiple such pairs of recievers / transmiters on each balloon, we could create a high density mesh network that can be deployed in the aid of a user in the middle of the ocean, which is nowere near any other form of radio / optical communication.

  BALLOONS

        The balloons travel above airplanes and weather events, at aproximately 20km.

        These balloons, exclusively, can fly above all oceans from a launch site.

        Predictive models of the winds and an autonomous decision-making algorithm moves each balup or down a layer blowing from the right direction.

        A group of balloons creates a network that provides WiFi to people in a range of 40km, in the same way a group of towers on the ground forms a terrestrial network.

        The only difference is that these specific air " towers " are constantly moving with the winds.

        Algorithms have the job of keeping the balloons at an aproximately exact position compared to the one they should be in in the first place, the range being always variable with less than 1km away.

        Every balis being tracked using GPS, constantly showing how far up it is and where, at any given moment.

         When a balis ready to be taken out of service, the lift gas keeping the balaloft is released and the flight capsule/basket lands in the water ( with the help of a parachute ).

        Descents are coordinated with the local air traffic control to land the balloon safely.

        Naval recovery teams then collect it by finding its beacon signal which activates when contact with water is made, for reusal purposes.

        The entire network can work fully automatically, efficiently routing connectivity across balloons and ground stations while taking in account balmotion, obstructions and weather events.


        How do they communicate between each other?

        There's an antenna that transmits signals everywhere, which allows a balloon to identify the position of other balloons and also to receive the information if the nearest flight capsule is communicating with has wifi or not, being able to transmit the data to a different one.


        How does the connectivity work?

     1. Wireless signal is transmited to the nearest bal

     2. Signal is sent from a balto the next closest bal

     3. Users connect to the mobile network through the balloons
